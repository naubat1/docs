## A guide to reading your dashboard

The Naubat dashboard is designed to help you **understand patterns and trends**, not to provide absolute or instantaneous diagnoses.

The information shown is based on **real usage data collected over time**, which means context is essential.

---

## Focus on trends, not single values

Individual trips or isolated data points can be influenced by many factors:
- Temperature
- Driving conditions
- Short journeys
- Vehicle state at that moment

For this reason, Naubat emphasizes **evolution over time** rather than single measurements.

---

## What the metrics are designed to tell you

Dashboard metrics are meant to help you:
- Understand how your vehicle is being used
- Identify changes in behavior or performance
- Compare usage patterns over time

They are **decision-support tools**, not fault detectors.

---

## Battery-related metrics: important considerations

Battery behavior naturally varies depending on:
- Operating temperature
- Driving style
- State of charge range
- Vehicle configuration

Seeing variation does **not automatically indicate a problem**.

Naubat helps you put these variations into context.

---

## When should you pay attention?

You should pay attention when you observe:
- Persistent trends over time
- Clear deviations from your usual patterns
- Changes that remain visible across different conditions

This is where the dashboard provides the most value.

---

## An independent and transparent approach

Naubat does not aim to replace manufacturer diagnostics.

Instead, it provides an **independent layer of insight**, helping you:
- Ask better questions
- Make informed decisions
- Avoid unnecessary assumptions

---

If you are unsure about a specific metric, we recommend reviewing it over time rather than drawing conclusions from a single snapshot.
